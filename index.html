FELICIA BISNATH

University of Michigan, Ann Arbor

I am a Trinidadian PhD student of linguistics at the University of Michigan (beginning fall 2019), Ann Arbor beginning Fall 2019 and advised by Dr. Savithry Namboodiripad and Dr. Natasha Abner. I first entered university doing a BSc in biochemistry at the University of the West Indies in Trinidad and Tobago. I did not complete this programme, but I did gain an interest in human evolution from it. I transferred into doing a BA in linguistics during which I focused on creoles, specifically Trinidadian French Creole. I then went on to do a MA at the University of Amsterdam where I primarily documented sign languages in Trinidad (verb typology and wh-questions). During my PhD I aim to use my background in language contact and sign linguistics, and associated fields like gesture, to add to our understanding of language evolution. I am also interested in the syntax of languages that are characteristically learned and used in contact with closely-related varieties.

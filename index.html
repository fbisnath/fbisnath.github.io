FELICIA BISNATH

/fəliːsjə bɪsnaθ/ | she/her

I am a first year PhD student of linguistics at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor advised by Dr. Savithry Namboodiripad. I have studied contact languages, specifically Caribbean creoles (Trinidadian French Creole, Sranan Tongo), and sign languages (Trinidad and Tobago Sign Language, Sign Language of the Netherlands).

During my PhD I plan to work on language evolution, gesture, and sign languages. Right now I'm doing an experimental syntax project on complementiser-trace effects in Trinidadian English Creole, and thinking about how semiotic resources, specifically mouthings and fingerspelling, are recruited into sign language systems.
